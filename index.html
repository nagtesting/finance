⚡

Power of Starting Early

₹5,000/month at 25 years old grows to ₹3.52 Cr by 60. Starting at 35 with the same SIP yields only ₹1.17 Cr — compounding rewards the early bird by 3x.

📐

Rule of 72

Divide 72 by your return rate to find when money doubles. At 12% return, your money doubles every 6 years. Start with ₹10L and in 24 years it becomes ₹1.6 Cr without adding another rupee.

📊

Rupee Cost Averaging

SIPs automatically buy more units when markets fall and fewer when high. This averaging strategy eliminates timing risk and has historically produced better risk-adjusted returns than lump sum investing.

🏦

Prepayment Strategy

Prepaying even 1 extra EMI per year on a ₹50L, 20-year loan at 8.5% can save ₹8-10 Lakhs in interest and reduce tenure by 2-3 years. Early prepayments have maximum impact as they reduce the principal on which interest compounds.

📉

Floating vs Fixed Rate

Floating rate loans track the RBI repo rate. When rates fall, your EMI drops — but rises when they increase. Fixed rates offer certainty but typically cost 0.5–1% more. In an easing cycle, floating wins; in a rising cycle, fixed is safer.

The 4% Safe Withdrawal Rule

Based on the Trinity Study (1998), withdrawing 4% of your corpus annually has historically sustained a portfolio for 30+ years across all market conditions. Your corpus target = Annual Expenses × 25.

🌿

Lean FIRE

Retire early with a minimal budget — typically expenses below ₹40K/month. Requires frugal living but provides freedom decades early. Popular among those valuing time over lifestyle.

🏆

Fat FIRE

Retire with a luxurious lifestyle — typically ₹1.5L+/month in expenses. Needs a corpus of ₹4–5 Cr+. Suited for high earners who don't want to compromise on lifestyle post-retirement.

⚖️

Barista FIRE

Semi-retire with a smaller corpus and cover the gap with part-time work. The balance between freedom and financial security — ideal for those who want to slow down but not fully stop.

🔄

CAGR vs Absolute Return

A fund with 100% absolute return over 10 years has only 7.2% CAGR — much less impressive. Always compare investments using CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) for apples-to-apples comparison across different time periods.

📈

Why Equity Beats FD Long-Term

FD at 7%: ₹1L becomes ₹3.87L in 20 years. Equity at 14%: ₹1L becomes ₹13.74L in 20 years — 3.5x more. Short-term volatility is the price you pay for long-term wealth creation.

📝

Term Plan vs ULIPs

Term plans offer pure life cover at low cost (~₹12,000/yr for ₹1 Cr cover at age 30). ULIPs mix insurance and investment — but rarely beat pure term + separate mutual fund. Keep insurance and investment separate.

🏥

Health Insurance First

Medical inflation runs at 14-18% in India. A single hospitalisation can wipe out years of savings. Get ₹10-20L health cover as a base + a super top-up for catastrophic coverage at low premiums.

⏰

Buy Young, Save Big

Term plan premiums double roughly every 10 years you delay. A ₹1 Cr cover costs ~₹8K/yr at 25, ~₹16K at 35, and ~₹32K+ at 45. Buying early locks in lifetime low premiums.
